Soba

I made this for lunch on a weekday, super quick and easy to make. Conveniently this is all stuff I've had for months, but your local, relatively not busy Asian supermarket should carry it all.

  1. Boil the soba noodles (buckwheat noodles) according to the instructions on the package, should be like 6 minutes.
  2. Take 2 parts water, 1 part soy sauce, 1 part mirin (Japanese cooking wine), a bit of dashi (fish stock) and boil it together. I did this in the microwave. For one person you need max like a total of a quarter cup.
  3. Cool noodles in cold water, cool sauce in the fridge.
  4. Drain noodles, pour sauce on top, enjoy with some nori (dried seaweed) if you have it on hand.

Granola

This recipe is from my mom. I haven't yet made it, but I'm about to.

Other breakfast items have been bacon and eggs (but now the eggs need conserving for more important things and the bacon goes in the freezer), instant pancake mix, oatmeal with frozen berries and now-precious milk, yogurt, a slice of plain bread, and the ever popular just waiting until lunch. Granola however is easy to make, or so my mom has always said.

  1. Heat 1/2 cup of canola oil and 1/2 cup honey in a metal pan until it bubbles at the edge
  2. Spread 4 cups of oats and whatever nuts and dried fruit you lkeon a baking pan
  3. Add the oil/honey mixture
  4. Bake until golden brown, maybe like 15 minutes
